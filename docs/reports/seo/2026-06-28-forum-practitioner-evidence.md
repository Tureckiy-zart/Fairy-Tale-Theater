## Recurring patterns

1. **SAB hidden-address penalty is the dominant theme (ANECDOTAL).** #1, #25, #26 converge: hiding the address (which Google *requires* for SABs) correlates with weaker local-pack visibility. Central tension for Miss Lana — comply with policy, treat the ranking impact as a known risk to mitigate via reviews/categories/content. **Correlation reported by practitioners, not a confirmed ranking factor.**
2. **SABs are structurally harder to rank than storefronts (ANECDOTAL).** #2, #6, #22 — success comes from nailing basics.
3. **City/landing pages help only when genuinely unique (ANECDOTAL).** #11–#14 — near-duplicate "city-swapped" pages are doorway pages; enforcement is inconsistent, which is *not* a green light.
4. **Primary GBP category is the strongest GBP lever and increasingly hard to change (ANECDOTAL).** #16 — choose carefully up front.
5. **Review acquisition: simple direct asks work; gating is the trap (ANECDOTAL).** #9, #10 — the "separate feedback from review" tactic shades into gating, a policy violation.
6. **New-site / Next.js indexing delay is a concrete launch failure mode (ANECDOTAL).** #17, #18 — perfect on-page score yet "Discovered – currently not indexed." Expect lag; ensure homepage indexed + strong internal linking.
7. **Domain migration / redirect mistakes cause real, lasting losses (ANECDOTAL).** #23, #24.
8. **AI Overviews reshaping local search in 2025-26 with high uncertainty (ANECDOTAL).** #20 — consensus only that pages must be indexed/snippet-eligible to be cited.
9. **Citations shifted from "consistency-critical" to "maintenance" (ANECDOTAL).** #7, #8.

## Contradictions & disagreements

- **Does hiding the address actually hurt?** #1 ("doesn't matter") vs #25/#26 (clear negative correlation). Weight the quantified case studies above casual opinion — but all remain anecdotal vs Google's own (contradictory) position that SABs aren't ranked differently.
- **Do thin city pages get penalized?** #13 (yes, doorway) vs #14 (enforcement "very inconsistent"). Safe reading: inconsistency is not permission.
- **NAP consistency weight?** #7 internal split.
- **"Separate feedback from review" — smart UX or gating?** #10.
- **Does GBP still matter in AI search?** #20 unresolved.

## Cross-check against official policy (per actionable pattern)

- **Hidden address for SABs:** Google Business Profile Help (answer/9157481) requires addressless SABs to hide the address; official position is SABs aren't ranked differently — *conflicts* with anecdotal evidence. **Action:** comply (no storefront → hiding is mandatory); mitigate via reviews/categories/content; **never** fake an address (suspension risk).
- **Verification & suspension:** GBP "Fix suspended profiles" (answer/4569145). **Action:** verify honestly via video (location + props/costumes + authorization); avoid edits right after verification.
- **City/landing pages:** Search Central doorway-pages spam policy. **Action:** build a geo page only with genuine, unique local substance; skip mass LA-suburb pages.
- **GBP categories:** "Improve your local ranking" (answer/7091). **Action:** pick the most accurate primary category first time; expect change requests may be blocked/appealed.
- **Reviews — no gating/incentives/fakes:** Maps prohibited-content policy; "Get more reviews" (answer/3474122). **Action:** ask *all* customers with a review link; never screen sentiment first; respond to all reviews.
- **Citations/NAP:** no Google doc mandates third-party citation consistency; ranking = relevance/distance/prominence. **Action:** keep NAP (esp. phone) accurate on a few high-authority directories; don't over-invest.
- **New-site / Next.js indexing:** GSC Page-indexing report (answer/7440203); JavaScript SEO basics. **Action:** ensure SSR/static HTML (App Router does this — verified in this audit), submit sitemap, confirm homepage indexed, expect a normal new-site delay.
- **Domain migration / redirects:** Site moves doc + Change of Address tool; keep 301s ≥1 year. **Action (if migrating):** 1:1 redirects, no chains, single canonical host, Change of Address, monitor both properties.
- **AI Overviews / AI search:** citation requires an indexed, snippet-eligible page (standard Search technical requirements); the May 15 2026 gen-AI guide states no extra requirements. **Action:** no special "AI SEO" lever — strong indexable content + GBP + reviews; monitor, don't chase unverified promises.
