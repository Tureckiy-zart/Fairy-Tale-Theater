### 1. Bob Baker Marionette Theater — DIRECT-ADJACENT (venue, not mobile)
- **URL:** bobbakermarionettetheater.com
- **Model:** Fixed **venue** (York Blvd, LA 90042). Nonprofit. NOT touring/mobile (has an "Around Town" offsite arm).
- **Offer:** Marionette/puppet performances, immersive shows, educational programs, workshops.
- **Presence (qualitative):** Very strong. LA Historic-Cultural Monument, Wikipedia + LA Conservancy coverage, operating since 1963, large Yelp/Facebook footprint. Heavy earned media / institutional authority.
- **Relevance to Miss Lana:** Puppets + venue model — a different product. Sets the high bar for *brand story/heritage* and *media authority*, not a direct booking competitor.

### 2. Puppet Theater on Wheels — DIRECT (mobile, but puppets)
- **URL:** puppettheateronwheels.com (Valley Village)
- **Model:** **Mobile** — "brings world-class puppet shows to your doorstep." Closest *business model* match (we-come-to-you), but **puppets**, not live costumed actors.
- **Offer:** Birthday parties, schools, daycares, festivals, corporate, public/outdoor events + workshops. ~1-hour total. **Shows start at $395** (travel fee may apply). Operating since 2012.
- **Presence (qualitative):** Solid. Established Yelp listing, active Facebook (counts not independently verifiable via WebSearch — owner/handoff to confirm).
- **Relevance:** The **most direct structural competitor**. Miss Lana's edge: live costumed actors + games/dancing/bubbles (not puppetry); price "from $350" (undercuts $395).

### 3. PollyBilly (Mobile Puppet Theater PollyBilly) — DIRECT (mobile, puppets, values-led)

## Defensible gaps Miss Lana can own (without copying anyone)

1. **"One brand, three jobs done well": touring + school-assembly + birthday/family — all as live costumed fairy-tale theatre.** No competitor cleanly unifies *live fairy-tale plays* across schools, birthdays, AND family events. That cross-cluster coherence is defensible.
2. **Live costumed actors, NOT puppets.** The closest *mobile* competitors (Puppet Theater on Wheels, PollyBilly) are puppet-based. "Real actors, real costumes, real play" is a clear, honest contrast.
3. **Early-years (ages 2-5) fairy-tale theatre for preschools/Montessori/daycare.** Assembly incumbents skew K-6; PollyBilly starts at 3. The age-2 floor + preschool/Montessori focus is under-served. (Win this with a strong /school-shows page, not thin geo pages.)
4. **"Bright, but kind" values framing — executed distinctly.** PollyBilly and Shows That Teach already use kindness/SEL language; tie kindness to the specific lantern/warm-light brand idea so the framing is ownable, not generic SEL.
5. **Real troupe + founder story.** A genuine "Miss Lana + the troupe" narrative on /about builds trust faceless party-marketplaces can't match.
6. **Transparent, competitive pricing ("from $350").** Benchmarks: Puppet Theater on Wheels from $395; California Kids Fun $375/45 min. Keep "From $350" crawlable on /pricing where many rivals hide pricing entirely.
